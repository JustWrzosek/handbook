Force Matrix

Definition: The Force Matrix is the amount of force used upon a suspect who is under the process of being arrested or detained.

These will now be enforced on an Administrative level. Faction members are required to follow the Force Matrix unless in the case it is heavily roleplayed and is not to the extreme. Use of pepper spray/nitestick is permitted if not used in a way of excessive force. It will be considered Non-Roleplay Behavior to not follow these; for example, to shoot upon an individual who by the Force Matrix would not be considered to have committed a crime that warrants such force.

Force Matrix Guidelines

Force Level 1: Suspect may be verbally aggressive.

1. Suspect may be showing aggression in his tone of speaking.
2. Suspect may be taunting you, or using foul languages.

Your actions: At this point in time, you are to issue verbal warnings or gestures to calm them down. No physical contact may occur.

Force Level 2: Suspect may have committed a crime.

1. Suspect may be disobeying orders from a LEO.
2. Suspect is hinting violent actions.
3. Suspect is making aggressive movements or gestures.
4. Suspect may be preparing for physical contact.
5. Suspect may be increasingly aggressive.
6. Suspect is showing disrespect.

Your actions: At this point in time you may not use secondary equipment. Call a Code 2 and move to a hands-on approach of attempting to escort the person away from the situation or potential victims using voice commands and light gestures.

Force Level 3: Suspect has committed a crime.

1. Suspect may be making physical contact.
2. Suspect may be using a non-lethal weapon.
3. Suspect may be threatening lives.
4. Suspect may be involved in a physical fight.
5. Suspect is trespassing onto private property or into a private government facility. (10 second warning required).
6. Suspect is trespassing into a barricaded area during an ongoing operation (a government announcement is required) (10 second warning is not required).

Your actions: At this time you may use a taser, Mace pepperspray or a nightstick to detain them. Call a Code 3 backup if necessary. Do not withdraw a firearm.

Force Level 4: Suspect has or is committing a felony.

1. Suspect is brandishing an illegal melee weapon or aiming a firearm.
2. Suspect may be actively aiming.
3. Suspect has placed lives in danger.
4. Suspect may have seriously assaulted somebody.
5. Suspect may have a hostage.

Your actions: At this time the suspect is threatening to end a human life. You may unholster and aim a firearm on the suspect. Do not fire unless the force reaches 5. Do not taser the suspect if they are actively firing upon you.

Force Level 5: Suspect is a Most Wanted felon.

1. Suspect is in the act of seriously assaulting someone.
2. Suspect is actively aiming a firearm at a Law Enforcement Officer.
3. Suspect may be firing at you or civilians.
4. Suspect is an escaped convict.
5. Suspect may be trespassing in Whitehouse or Area 51.
6. Suspect is brandishing an illegal firearm and trespassing into a barricaded area during an ongoing operation (a government announcement is required).
7. Suspect is hospitalized and has revealed a weapon upon leaving the hospital.

Your actions: At this point you may open fire at the suspect and shoot to kill. Attempt to avoid such a situation if possible. You must get additional units on-scene.

Vehicle Force Matrix Guidelines

Vehicle Force Level 1: Suspect is recklessly driving.

Your actions: Signal for the suspect to pull over to the side and switch off the vehicle engine.

Vehicle Force Level 2: Suspect is not responding to signal.

1. Suspect is evading Law Enforcement in vehicle.

Your actions: Call a Code 3 backup for a vehicle pursuit. Pursue the suspect until additional Traffic units arrive.

Vehicle Force Level 3: Suspect is evading multiple Law Enforcement units in vehicle.

1. Suspect endangering the public with reckless driving and evading.
2. Suspect has attempted to escape a scene of the crime.

Your actions: Attempt to P.I.T. maneuver the suspect off the road and disable the suspect's vehicle.

Vehicle Force Level 4: Suspect is evading multiple Law Enforcement units in vehicle.

1. Suspect endangering the public with reckless driving and evading.
2. Suspect has successfully evaded attempted P.I.T. maneuvers.
3. Suspect has hostage(s) in vehicle.
4. Suspect may be ramming or slamming other vehicles with his own.

Your actions: Set up roadblocks ahead of the evading vehicle. Road-spikes and barricades may be used. Do NOT open fire at this level.

Vehicle Force Level 5: Suspect is firing upon pursuing Law Enforcement units from vehicle.

1. Suspect is firing upon the public from vehicle.
2. Suspect is fleeing away in an aircraft.
3. Suspect may be vehicular assaulting you or civilians (on foot).
4. Suspect has actively evaded in a vehicle for over 20 minutes (to be calculated from when the first charge is placed).
5. Suspect is fleeing away in a boat.
6. Suspect is using a large vehicle to severely damage your vehicle with their own repeatedly.

Large Vehicles include: Linerunner, Dumper, Bus, Barracks, Packer, Flatbed, Yankee, Dozer, Tanker, Roadtrain, Cement truck, Combine Harvester, Duneride, DFT-30, Trashmaster, Sandking

7. Suspect is flying above a residential area, crowded place or a government facility with low altitude. (Must be warned to leave the area and given 10 seconds to do so before using gunfire.)

Your actions: You may retaliate with gunfire, take out the tires before going for the vehicle's engine. *You may only shoot at a fleeing boat with a Police Predator.* In any case, ensuring the public's safety is your top priority.
