Chain of Commands (CoC)

Trainee Investigator:
• Completing basic training programs.
• Learning investigative techniques and procedures.
• Shadowing experienced investigators.

Junior Investigator:
• Assisting senior investigators with cases.
• Conducting initial interviews and gathering evidence.
• Preparing reports and documentation.

Investigator:
• Conducting independent investigations.
• Interviewing witnesses and suspects.
• Analyzing evidence and compiling case files.

Senior Investigator:
• Leading complex investigations.
• Supervising junior investigators.
• Liaising with other law enforcement agencies.

Assistant Special Agent:
• Specializing in specific areas of investigation (e.g., narcotics, organized crime).
• Managing teams of investigators.
• Assisting in high-profile cases.

Special Agent:
• Conducting sensitive and high-priority investigations.
• Collaborating with federal and international agencies.
• Testifying in court as an expert witness.

Senior Special Agent:
• Leading major investigations with significant impact.
• Overseeing multiple teams of agents.
• Providing expertise and guidance to lower-ranked agents.

Assistant Chief Investigator:
• Assisting in the management and administration of investigative units.
• Developing strategies for combating crime.
• Representing the agency in meetings and conferences.

Chief Investigator:
• Directing all investigative activities within the agency.
• Setting priorities and allocating resources.
• Formulating policies and procedures.

Assistant Director:
• Assisting in the overall management of the agency.
• Supervising multiple divisions or units.
• Representing the agency to external stakeholders.

Deputy Director:
• Assuming responsibility for the agency in the absence of the director.
• Overseeing day-to-day operations.
• Advising the director on strategic matters.

Director General:
• Providing overall leadership and direction to the agency.
• Setting the agency's mission and vision.
• Representing the agency at the highest levels of government and in international forums.
